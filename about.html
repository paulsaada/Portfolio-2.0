What are my skills and how did I get them?

Back in 2008, I started out on my developer journey crudely editing the HTML and CSS on a small tech blog hosted on Blogspot.

Since those experiences from my younger years, I've kept coding and solving challenging new problems. As of now, front-end development is my primary area of expertise, while my job requires me to regularly venture into back-end territory to develop new features or maintain current ones.

With the support of my wonderful coworkers at my current role, coupled with the leadership of ~20 year developer veteran/my mentor/tech lead, Henry Richardson, my growth has been exponential.

Through my experiences at Interactive Nerd, I've gained a new level of mastery of React, I lead the development efforts for the official launch of the company's website, I developed a full-stack social media content automation platform called Creatr Pro, and I created a bunch of cool React games for fun.

Some of my greatest strengths that make me an integral part of the teams I join are:

  1. My ability to listen and accept professional criticism from my colleagues about my work--This is what makes me stronger!
  2. My ability to learn new concepts and ideas quickly, speeding up the initial onboarding process as well as any future migrations to new frameworks/libraries
  3. My willingness to step up, communicate, and take charge of situations when things go wrong
